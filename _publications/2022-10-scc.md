abstract: 'Modern processors frequently execute micro-operations (&micro;ops) whose results are never consumed&mdash;dead &micro;ops that waste execution resources without contributing to program output. We propose Speculative Code Compaction (SCC), a microarchitectural technique that speculatively identifies and eliminates dead &micro;ops using dynamic dataflow analysis. SCC reduces back-end pressure and improves instruction throughput transparently to software, achieving meaningful speedups across a range of real-world workloads.'
